Heavy-Ion Collision Papers

Theory

Axial-Vector Lattice Benchmarks Reveal a Common Medium Response of Meson Screening in Hot QCD

Strong

Jie Ren, Chen Chen, Fei Gao, Si-xue Qin. arXiv:2606.26245

The paper connects hot-QCD meson screening masses to a symmetry-preserving Dyson-Schwinger baseline, using one axial-vector lattice point to fix the medium response and leaving other axial-vector and vector channels as validation tests. The flavor-dependent screening predictions give a structured way to move between lattice information and model spectra.

Why it matters: Screening masses are clean finite-temperature QCD diagnostics and help constrain how hadronic correlations dissolve above the crossover.

Critical assessment: Strong within its theory scope. The external lattice anchors and held-out validation make it more convincing than a purely tuned model, though direct heavy-ion phenomenology remains downstream.

Applicability of kinetic theory in strongly coupled thermal quantum systems

Mixed

Shile Chen, Shuzhe Shi, Pengfei Zhuang. arXiv:2606.26696

The authors study finite-temperature single- and two-particle momentum distributions in simplified strongly interacting spinor systems, including Schwinger and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio examples. They argue that at sufficiently high temperature, when thermal kinetic energy becomes comparable with the interaction scale, two-particle correlations become subdominant enough for a kinetic description.

Why it matters: Heavy-ion transport and hydrodynamic afterburner modeling lean on kinetic assumptions, so controlled diagnostics of correlation suppression are conceptually useful.

Critical assessment: Interesting but indirect. The simplified systems make this a useful stress test of kinetic intuition, not validation of any particular QGP transport code.

Holographic light-quark energy loss in a spinning plasma

Mixed

Yan-Qing Zhao, Zhou-Run Zhu. arXiv:2606.26598

This holographic study computes light-quark stopping distance and instantaneous energy loss in a spinning black-brane plasma using falling-string and shooting-string probes. It finds shorter stopping distances and stronger energy loss with increasing temperature or rotation parameter, with a larger effect for transverse motion than motion along the rotation axis.

Why it matters: The work targets anisotropic jet-quenching physics in rotating or vortical plasma backgrounds, a plausible conceptual neighbor of heavy-ion angular-momentum and vorticity studies.

Critical assessment: Useful as a qualitative strong-coupling diagnostic, but model distance from QCD is large. The result should not be treated as a quantitative jet-quenching prediction.

Phenomenology

Studying the QCD Matter produced in Heavy-Ion Collisions using the MUSES Calculation Engine

Strong

Johannes Jahan, Kevin P. Pala, Yumu Yang, Isabella Danhoni, Prachi Garella, et al.; 45 listed authors, MUSES Collaboration. arXiv:2606.26326

The Calliope release of the MUSES Calculation Engine is presented as a finite-density equation-of-state and workflow platform for heavy-ion modeling. It includes EoS modules across lattice-QCD and phenomenological inputs, extensions in baryon, strangeness, and electric-charge chemical potentials, thermodynamic derivatives, transport-coefficient interfaces, EoS merging, EoS inversion, and example viscous-hydrodynamic simulations at 7.7, 19.6, and 39 GeV.

Why it matters: Reproducible finite-density EoS workflows are central for Beam Energy Scan interpretation, especially when testing critical-point scenarios and propagating EoS choices into hydrodynamics.

Critical assessment: Strong infrastructure paper. The example simulations are demonstrations rather than final phenomenological constraints, and the real impact will depend on public code availability, documentation, and adoption in model-to-data pipelines.

Rapidity-even directed flow splitting of protons and antiprotons as a probe of baryon stopping in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

Mixed

Tribhuban Parida, Sandeep Chatterjee. arXiv:2606.27156

Using 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics coupled to hadronic transport, this paper studies rapidity-even directed-flow splitting between protons and antiprotons at BES energies. The proposed midrapidity curvature of the splitting is presented as a discriminator of baryon deposition profiles and baryon diffusion strength.

Why it matters: Baryon stopping is a major initial-condition uncertainty at finite baryon density, and the observable is experimentally concrete.

Critical assessment: Focused and useful, but not yet decisive. Precision data and careful treatment of afterburner, diffusion, and initial-state degeneracies will be needed before the observable can sharply constrain stopping.

AI/ML Use Cases in Physics

Interpreting "Interpretability" and Explaining "Explainability" in Machine Learning in Physics

Strong

Rikab Gambhir, Luisa Lucie-Smith, Jesse Thaler. arXiv:2606.26228

Method and result: This review separates interpretability, meaning model transparency, from explainability, meaning a defensible connection to scientific domain knowledge. It emphasizes task specification and intervention planning as part of physics ML design.

Likely HEP/heavy-ion use case: The framing transfers directly to event classification, unfolding, detector calibration, anomaly detection, simulation tuning, and surrogate modeling where ML claims need to be auditable.

Critical assessment: Strong and broadly transferable. It is conceptual rather than algorithmic, but that is valuable because HEP and nuclear-physics ML papers often use interpretability language loosely.

Supercool with PPO: Exploring Supercooled Phase Transitions via Reinforcement Learning

Mixed

Wan-Zhe Feng, Zong-Huan Ye, Zi-Hui Zhang. arXiv:2606.26251

Method and result: The authors build a Proximal Policy Optimization environment to search model-parameter regions with detectable gravitational-wave signals from supercooled first-order phase transitions, comparing goal-directed RL scans with conventional Monte Carlo scans.

Likely HEP/heavy-ion use case: The transferable idea is not the specific dark-sector model, but reward-guided exploration of sparse, high-dimensional physics parameter spaces, such as emulator tuning, transport-coefficient scans, or rare observable optimization.

Critical assessment: Promising but mixed. The method is concrete and reusable, but the strength depends on reward design and on whether the RL search preserves coverage of physically important but lower-reward regions.

Optimal Transport Event Representation for Anomaly Detection

Strong

Tianji Cai, Aditya Bhargava, Benjamin Nachman. arXiv:2512.04839 (replacement)

Method and result: The paper introduces optimal transport as a physics-informed intermediate event representation for weakly supervised anomaly detection. On LHC Olympics benchmarks with a small injected signal fraction, OT-augmented features nearly double the significance improvement of standard high-level observables in the reported idealized setup.

Likely HEP/heavy-ion use case: Structured event representations could help searches, jet substructure, rare topology discovery, and potentially heavy-ion anomaly or outlier studies where low-level end-to-end learning is brittle.

Critical assessment: Strong for HEP ML methodology, with the usual caveat that benchmark gains need stress tests under detector effects, background mismodeling, and more realistic training constraints.

Machine Learning-based Unfolding for Cross Section Measurements in the Presence of Nuisance Parameters

Strong

Huanbiao Zhu, Krish Desai, Mikael Kuusela, Vinicius Mikuni, Benjamin Nachman, Larry Wasserman. arXiv:2512.07074 (replacement)

Method and result: The paper extends OmniFold into Profile OmniFold, incorporating nuisance parameters into ML-based unfolding. It is demonstrated on a Gaussian example and a simulated CMS case study.

Likely HEP/heavy-ion use case: This is directly relevant to differential measurements where detector corrections, model uncertainties, centrality dependence, and correlated systematics must be profiled rather than ignored.

Critical assessment: Strong methodological relevance. Heavy-ion deployment would need stress tests under high multiplicity, background subtraction, centrality selection, and generator-prior mismodeling.

Weak or Overclaimed Papers Worth Caution

Kinetic freeze-out and diffusion dynamics in small-system asymmetric collisions at sqrt(sNN)=200 GeV in light of a generalized Fokker-Planck distribution

Caution

M. Waqas, Wolfgang Bietenholz, Khusniddin K. Olimov, Muhammad Ajaz, Jihane Ben Slimane, Laila A. Al-Essa, A. Haj Ismail. arXiv:2606.26301

The paper fits neutral-pion spectra in p-Al, p-Au, d-Au, and He3-Au collisions with a generalized Fokker-Planck distribution and interprets trends in fitted parameters as freeze-out and diffusion dynamics.

Why caution: The small-system connection is relevant, but the inference leans on flexible spectral fits. Claims about QGP sensitivity need stronger baseline comparisons, uncertainties, and independent observables.

Thermodynamical analysis of QGP using effective PNJL model with Quasiparticle approach

Caution

Yogesh Kumar, Himanshu Aggarwal, R. S. Laishram, Poonam Jain, Om Prakash, et al.; 10 authors. arXiv:2606.26956

The paper extends a two-flavor PNJL setup with quasiparticle masses and reports improved agreement with lattice-QCD thermodynamic observables at zero chemical potential and zero magnetic field.

Why caution: It may be a useful model baseline, but the abstract signals mostly fit improvement. Without independent validation or uncertainty propagation, it should not be read as a robust equation-of-state advance.

The Effect of Topological Defects and Magnetic Flux on Fully-Heavy Tetraquarks and Mass Spectra of Heavy Quarkonia Using the Analytical Exact Iteration Method

Caution

N. H. Gerish, M. Abu-Shady, E. M. Khokha. arXiv:2606.27363

The paper solves a modified Cornell-potential model with topological-defect and magnetic-flux terms and reports mass spectra for heavy quarkonia and fully heavy tetraquarks.

Why caution: The abstract makes strong precision language from a model potential calculation. For heavy-ion or QCD-medium purposes, it is too indirect and too model-dependent to prioritize.

Magnetic moments of decuplet baryons in isospin asymmetric magnetized strange matter

Caution

Hrishika P, Manpreet Kaur, Suneel Dutt, Harleen Dahiya, Arvind Kumar. arXiv:2606.26759

The paper computes in-medium decuplet-baryon masses and magnetic moments in hot, dense, magnetized strange matter using a chiral SU(3) quark mean-field model combined with a chiral constituent quark model.

Why caution: It touches dense and magnetized QCD matter, but the connection to heavy-ion collision observables is indirect. It is better treated as model-specific hadron-structure input than as a heavy-ion physics result.
