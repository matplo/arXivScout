Daily arXiv Physics Scout

2026-06-29 Briefing

Selective review of recent arXiv papers relevant to heavy-ion collision physics, QCD matter, collider/nuclear phenomenology, and AI/ML methods with credible HEP or nuclear-physics transfer value.

Executive Takeaways

  • QCD phase-structure papers dominated the heavy-ion-relevant theory stream: one maps a proposed critical surface in full chemical-potential space, while another performs a substantial lattice study of three-flavor QCD with Mobius domain-wall fermions.
  • The ALICE pp femtoscopy measurement is experimentally strong and directly useful for small-system and hadron-interaction analyses, especially because it tests multiplicity and transverse-mass dependence with upgraded-detector data.
  • AI/ML work was strongest where the learning model is tied to physics structure: symmetry-informed nuclear mass models, neural-network TMD extraction, and collider spectral-function event representations.
  • Several ML papers have transfer value but should not be over-read: the quantum generative calorimeter study is detector-relevant but still a specialized validation, while the PINN inverse-problem work is methodologically relevant but not yet HEP-specific.
  • A hidden-charm pentaquark sum-rule paper carried an arXiv text-overlap note; given the target scope and overlap flag, it is worth caution rather than prioritization today.

Heavy-Ion Collision Papers

Theory

QCD critical surface from constant entropy contours

strong

Hitansh Shah, Tristan Gyure, Anabella Leon, Francesco Di Clemente, Mauricio Hippert, Claudia Ratti, Volodymyr Vovchenko. arXiv:2606.28282

The paper extends a constant-entropy construction of the QCD critical point into the full (T, mu_B, mu_Q, mu_S) chemical-potential space. Its headline result is that the heavy-ion-relevant strangeness-neutral direction shifts the critical baryochemical potential upward by roughly 40-100 MeV while leaving the critical temperature nearly unchanged.

Why it matters: This is directly relevant for mapping BES-style trajectories and for translating critical-point estimates into experimentally constrained conserved-charge conditions.

Critical assessment: Strong and timely, but the expansion is truncated at O(mu^2), so the claimed critical surface should be treated as a controlled model construction rather than a definitive lattice result at large chemical potential.

The QCD phase diagram for three-flavor Mobius domain-wall fermions

strong

Yu Zhang, Yasumichi Aoki, Jishnu Goswami, Shoji Hashimoto, Issaku Kanamori, Takashi Kaneko, Yoshifumi Nakamura. arXiv:2606.28086

This 53-page lattice-QCD study investigates the three-degenerate-flavor transition at zero baryon chemical potential using Mobius domain-wall fermions, emphasizing chiral-symmetry preservation. The authors scan quark masses, volumes, and temporal extents and find finite-size behavior consistent with crossover at the studied mass points.

Why it matters: Better-controlled chiral fermions sharpen the connection between lattice phase-structure results and the QCD-matter baselines used in heavy-ion phenomenology.

Critical assessment: Methodologically solid and substantial. The scope is still zero mu_B and specific lattice spacings/volumes, so the main value is as a phase-structure benchmark, not an immediate phenomenological input for finite-density heavy-ion trajectories.

Experiment

Multiplicity dependence of the size of the common hadron emission source in pp collisions at the LHC

strong

ALICE Collaboration. arXiv:2606.28098

ALICE reports proton-proton correlation measurements in 13.6 TeV pp collisions using the 2022 upgraded-detector data set, corresponding to 19.3 pb^-1. The work extracts source radii versus multiplicity and transverse mass and tests robustness against several modern nucleon-nucleon interaction models.

Why it matters: Femtoscopic source sizes in pp are an essential baseline for residual strong-interaction measurements and for comparing small systems with Pb-Pb emission dynamics.

Critical assessment: Strong experimental contribution. The comparison to Pb-Pb reveals meaningful system-size dependence, although interpretation will still depend on how models separate source geometry, dynamics, and final-state interaction assumptions.

Phenomenology

Antideuteron production from beauty-hadron decays: a first phenomenological study

mixed

Marta Razza, Nicolo Jacazio, Francesca Bellini. arXiv:2606.27910

The authors estimate antideuteron production from anti-Lambda_b and B- decays using PYTHIA kinematics and a quantum-mechanical coalescence model based on an Argonne v18 antideuteron wave function. The predicted yields fall in a kinematic region accessible to ALICE.

Why it matters: It links collider antinucleus production, coalescence modeling, and indirect-dark-matter backgrounds, with a concrete experimental target.

Critical assessment: Useful and well-motivated as a first benchmark, but uncertainty is dominated by hadronization and coalescence modeling choices. Treat the branching-ratio ranges as targets for measurement, not settled predictions.

AI/ML Use Cases In Physics

Bridging Ab Initio Symmetries and Global Nuclear Masses with Interpretable Neural Networks

strong

Phong Dang, Evander Espinoza, Xiaoliang Wan, Michela Negro, Jerry P. Draayer, Feng Pan, Tomas Dytrych, Daniel Langr, David Kekejian. arXiv:2606.28287

The paper builds neural-network and compact mass-formula models from SU(3) and SU(4) Casimir operators, training on AME2016 and validating on nuclei new to AME2020. The compact Wigner-informed model reports a 0.430 MeV validation RMSE and highlights possible symmetry behavior near the neutron dripline and superheavy region.

Likely use case: Interpretable surrogate modeling for nuclear masses, extrapolation diagnostics, and physics-constrained uncertainty estimates in nuclear-structure workflows.

Critical assessment: Strong because the ML representation is anchored in explicit nuclear symmetries rather than generic feature fitting. The most speculative interpretive claims, especially symmetry restoration/global governance, deserve follow-up against broader baselines and uncertainty decompositions.

Neural-Network extraction of TMDs with SIDIS data

mixed

Matteo Cerutti. arXiv:2606.28218

This proceedings paper reports a first global unpolarized TMD extraction with neural-network parametrization using DY and SIDIS data at N^3LL accuracy. Including SIDIS broadens extracted TMD PDFs relative to DY-only fits and reduces uncertainties, though uncertainties remain larger than traditional model fits.

Likely use case: Flexible, lower-bias TMD inference for EIC and JLab phenomenology, with a workflow that can transfer to nuclear-modified TMD studies.

Critical assessment: Promising but thin as a 5-page proceedings contribution. The scientific weight depends on the full analysis details, baseline comparisons, and uncertainty methodology beyond the abstract-level summary.

Machine learning fully hadronic events with spectral functions

strong

Mohammad Mahdi Altakach, Hadi Hassan, Sabine Kraml, Kazuki Sakurai, Haitham Zaraket. arXiv:2606.27420

The paper proposes two-point correlation spectral functions as fixed-length ML inputs for variable-multiplicity fully hadronic collider events. In a gluino-pair benchmark against fully hadronic ttbar, the method improves projected reach relative to both a recent ATLAS analysis and a kinematics-only dense network.

Likely use case: Permutation-safe, radiation-tolerant event representations for HEP searches; potentially useful for jet-substructure and high-multiplicity heavy-ion jet studies where variable object counts are a persistent nuisance.

Critical assessment: Strong transfer value because the representation encodes collider symmetries and variable multiplicity explicitly. The main caveat is benchmark specificity; robustness under detector effects, pileup/underlying event, and domain shifts needs broader testing.

Qudit extension of parameterized IQP circuits: A generative quantum machine learning approach to integer data

mixed

Robert J. Banks, Arianna Crippa, Matthias Traube, Josua Unger, Christian Ertler, Wolfgang Lechner. arXiv:2606.28236

The authors extend parameterized IQP circuits toward integer-valued non-binary data and validate the generative model on single-particle electron-shower energy deposits in the CLIC electromagnetic calorimeter. The aim is to preserve metric structure that can be damaged by binary encodings.

Likely use case: Detector-simulation compression or generative modeling for calorimeter-like integer/quantized data, if quantum generative models become practically competitive.

Critical assessment: Worth tracking, but not yet a strong practical HEP-ML result. The validation is detector-relevant, yet the case for advantage over strong classical generative baselines remains the key missing discriminator.

Recovering Sharp Conductivity Features in the Finite-Data Calderon Problem with Physics-Informed Neural Networks

mixed

Ali AlHadi Kalout, Pablo Tejerina-Perez, Konstantin Karchev, Pedro Tarancon-Alvarez, Leonid Sarieddine, Raul Jimenez, Max Engelstein, Guy David. arXiv:2606.28158

This PINN paper studies finite-data inverse reconstruction with multiscale boundary excitations and Fourier-feature encodings, reporting roughly 3-12 percent relative errors on synthetic conductivity fields with inclusions, interfaces, and smooth profiles.

Likely use case: Transferable ideas for PDE-constrained inverse problems in detector calibration, tomography-like reconstruction, and simulation-constrained inference in nuclear/HEP contexts.

Critical assessment: Methodologically relevant but indirect. The synthetic finite-difference setting is useful for controlled validation, but HEP transfer would require realistic noise, model mismatch, and comparison to established inverse-problem solvers.

Weak Or Overclaimed Papers Worth Caution

Analysis of the hidden-charm pentaquark candidates in the J/psi Xi* mass spectrum via the QCD sum rules

caution

Zhi-Gang Wang. arXiv:2606.28095

The title may look relevant to hadron spectroscopy, but it is not central to today's heavy-ion or physics-ML scope, and the arXiv listing carries an admin note for text overlap with three earlier arXiv papers. I would not prioritize it without a specific spectroscopy need and a careful comparison to the overlapping works.
