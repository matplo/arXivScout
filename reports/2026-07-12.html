Heavy-Ion Collision Papers

Theory

Azimuthal momentum isotropization in the Quark-Gluon Plasma thermalization

strong

Sergio Barrera Cabodevila, Xiaojian Du, Carlos A. Salgado, Bin Wu · arXiv:2607.07796 · Theory

Studies relaxation of initial azimuthal momentum anisotropies using the Boltzmann Equation in Diffusion Approximation. It reports a hierarchy of relaxation times by harmonic and pT-dependent harmonic evolution that can mimic small-system correlation trends in a phenomenological simulation.

Why it matters: Initial-state momentum anisotropy is a live systematic in interpreting small-system collectivity; this gives a more quantitative relaxation diagnostic.

Critical assessment: Strong abstract-level signal. The next test is robustness in event-by-event initial conditions and with realistic hadronic afterburners.

A symmetry-based resolution of pseudo-gauge ambiguities in local equilibrium

strong

Carlos Hoyos · arXiv:2607.08569 · Theory

Reformulates pseudo-gauge and current-improvement ambiguities as spurious symmetries associated with currents of vanishing total charge, then proposes using genuine-symmetry currents to define the local-equilibrium density operator.

Why it matters: Spin polarization and vector-meson alignment remain interpretation-limited observables; reducing convention dependence is valuable theory infrastructure.

Critical assessment: Conceptually strong, but phenomenological weight depends on follow-up calculations showing numerical impact on heavy-ion spin observables.

Experiment

Recent highlights from the STAR Experiment

mixed

Rutik Manikandhan · arXiv:2607.07254 · Experiment

Proceedings overview of recent STAR results on mean-pT correlations, fourth-order net-proton cumulants, identical-pion femtoscopy, baryon-strangeness correlations, and baryon-baryon femtoscopy over sqrt(sNN)=3-200 GeV.

Why it matters: These observables map directly onto fluctuation, femtoscopy, and interaction constraints used in BES phenomenology.

Critical assessment: Useful status report, but the evidentiary strength lies in the underlying STAR measurements rather than new analysis in this proceeding.

Phenomenology

Analysis of Nuclear Fragmentation Functions for Pions with A and nu Dependence

strong

Mengyang Li, Zijian Ye, Jun Gao, Xiaomin Shen, Hongxi Xing, Yuxiang Zhao · arXiv:2607.08669 · Phenomenology

Fits pion nuclear fragmentation functions with nuclear effects parameterized by mass number A, target-rest-frame parton energy nu, and hadron fraction z, using nuclear SIDIS data and NLO pp/pA comparisons to ALICE.

Why it matters: Better nFF baselines reduce ambiguity between cold-nuclear hadronization effects and hot-medium modifications.

Critical assessment: Strong baseline work, though current pA comparisons remain uncertainty-limited and the fit is best constrained in a restricted z range.

Heavy quark coalescence probability in the presence of a potential

mixed

Taesoo Song, Jiaxing Zhao · arXiv:2607.08707 · Phenomenology

Studies how a phenomenological heavy-quark potential affects coalescence probabilities and finds near-unity low-momentum coalescence under moderate in-medium potential modification.

Why it matters: Heavy-flavor flow and baryon/meson ratios are sensitive to hadronization prescriptions.

Critical assessment: Useful model-ingredient study, but not yet a settled prescription without broader validation and uncertainty propagation.

AI/ML use

Optimal-Transport-Based Cell Resampling for Negative and Pathological Event Weights

strong

Regan Doherty, Lauren Hay, Rishabh Jain, Matt LeBlanc, Julia Marrinan, Camille Mauceri, Jennifer Roloff · arXiv:2607.08723 · AI/ML use

Uses optimal-transport metrics for local resampling of negative and very large Monte Carlo event weights, reporting reduced observed bias in NLO QCD samples relative to earlier cell-resampling techniques.

Likely use case: Precision collider simulations and hard-probe baseline production where negative weights create storage, statistics, and computing pressure.

Critical assessment: Strong and practical, though preliminary; scaling and detector/reconstruction robustness remain key checks.

Transformer-based machine learning using low-level calorimeter signals for collimated photon identification at collider experiments

strong

Gabriel Matos, Lauren Larson, Abhilasha Dave, Maria Bressan, Azal Amer, et al. (11 authors) · arXiv:2607.08175 · AI/ML use

Compares high-level and cell-level ML architectures for collimated diphoton versus isolated-photon identification; a Transformer performs best and also regresses invariant mass from calorimeter cells.

Likely use case: Photon/pi0 separation, shower reconstruction, trigger-oriented alternatives, and possible high-occupancy heavy-ion calorimetry studies after detector-specific validation.

Critical assessment: Strong method comparison with clear collider relevance; transfer to heavy-ion conditions needs pileup/occupancy tests.

The Generalization Gap in Machine Learning EoS Inference from Core-Collapse Supernova Gravitational Waves

strong

Ayan Mitra · arXiv:2607.06736 · AI/ML use

Shows that random cross-validation can make EoS inference appear successful, while leave-one-EoS-out validation fails badly across multiple model classes.

Likely use case: Validation design for heavy-ion EoS emulators, transport-parameter inference, and event-generator surrogates trained on finite model families.

Critical assessment: Strong as a negative-result methodology paper because it diagnoses template leakage and proposes a more realistic validation standard.

Weak Or Overclaimed Papers Worth Caution

Secondary Hadron-Nucleus Collisions of Short-Lived Hadrons in Ultra-Relativistic Fixed-Target Heavy-Ion Interactions

caution

Sanatan Digal, P. S. Saumia, Ajit M. Srivastava · arXiv:2607.08658 · Caution

Argues that boosted short-lived hadrons from one fixed-target heavy-ion interaction may survive to collide with another nucleus in a solid target.

Why it looks interesting: If viable, it would open unusual secondary hadron-nucleus projectile species.

Why caution is warranted: The concept is clever, but the path from survival probabilities to clean, measurable observables is not yet convincing from the available evidence.
